### AI Response

Yes, that's a **great idea** — but with a specific approach to make it actually useful:

#### ✅ Why it's smart
- The AI can't read your physical textbook. If the chapters are in your project as `.md` files, it can **reference exact Sebesta terminology and section numbers** when helping you write D1 or prepare for exams.
- Your project is graded on using **Sebesta's vocabulary precisely** — having the source material available means the AI can help you use the right terms in the right context.

#### ⚠️ But do it as concise summaries, not full copies
Instead of copying entire chapters word-for-word (which is a lot of text and possibly a copyright issue), create **condensed reference sheets** with:

1. **Key definitions** — e.g., "Static scoping (§5.5): scope determined by program structure, not execution"
2. **Key section numbers** — so we can cite them (e.g., §6.12 for strong typing)
3. **Key design questions** the book raises — these map directly to what D1 asks you to answer
4. **Important examples** from the book that clarify concepts

#### 📁 Suggested structure

```
new_version/
  Sebesta_References/
    Ch1_Preliminaries.md
    Ch3_Syntax.md
    Ch4_Lexical_Semantic.md
    Ch5_Names_Bindings_Scope.md
    Ch6_Data_Types.md
    Ch7_Expressions.md
```

#### 💡 Bonus benefit
Writing these summaries yourself doubles as exam prep — you're allowed 3 handwritten A4 sheets in each exam. These summaries would be perfect source material for your cheat sheets.
